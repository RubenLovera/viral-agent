## Executive Summary

Four weeks in, the SkinQueens UGC campaign has built a real foundation: **446 posts published**, **132,173 total views**, **4.84% engagement rate** — well above the 3–5% UGC industry benchmark — across a network of 31 active creators and 44 signed contracts.

The data tells an important story: **63% of all campaign views (87,149 of 132,173) came in the last 7 days alone.** The algorithm is catching up. Accounts that spent weeks warming up are now receiving organic distribution, and the most recent videos are outperforming everything that came before them. We are at the inflection point.

We came into this campaign targeting 1 million views. We are at 132K. That gap is real, and we own it.

Here's what we're doing about it: **The Viral App is committing one additional week of service at no cost to SkinQueens**, with a second week available if needed. No invoice. No negotiation. We're invested in seeing this campaign deliver, and the momentum we've built over four weeks gives us a legitimate shot at compressing significant results into the next two weeks with the right formats and the right creators.

The research we've done this week on competitor formats — OnSkin, Lóvi — reveals clear, replicable playbooks we haven't fully deployed yet. Week 5 is about executing those with the creators who have proven they can perform.

## Top 5 Best Performing Formats — Our Campaign

These are the formats that have proven themselves in the campaign data and are being replicated with other creators in Week 5.

### 🥇 Format 1 — Talking Head: Direct Recommendation
**Best example:** @eliseskincare (Elise Klepper, TikTok)  
**Hook:** *"She told me to use @SkinQueenApp!"*  
**Stats:** 3,717 views · 679 likes · **19.45% ER** — campaign's highest ER video  
**Format:** Creator speaks directly to camera. Recommends the app without hesitation. Paid partnership disclosed (#paidads). No complex demo, no props — just energy and authenticity.  
**Why it works:** The simplest format scales the best in UGC. Authenticity + disclosure builds trust. The social proof framing ("she told me") lowers resistance.  
**Replicating with:** All TikTok creators with active accounts.

---

### 🥈 Format 2 — Anti-Impulse Hook: "STOP Buying"
**Best example:** @serumshin_ (Shin Shields, IG) + @serumshin (TikTok, 13.49% ER)  
**Hook:** *"STOP buying every product you see on your fyp!!"*  
**Stats:** 1,624 views IG · 13.49% TikTok ER across account  
**Format:** "STOP" pattern interrupt → problem framing (you're wasting money) → SkinQueens as the rational solution.  
**Why it works:** Contrarian positioning creates instant curiosity. Every skincare buyer has wasted money on products that didn't work — this hook is universally relatable. Positions the app as the smart alternative.  
**Replicating with:** Shin Shields (scale), Kenna Conner, creators with product-review content.

---

### 🥉 Format 3 — Emotional Discovery: "I'm So Happy I Found This"
**Best example:** @cosmiccluvv (Bethany Vasquez, TikTok)  
**Hook:** *"I'm so happy I found this app to help my skin"*  
**Stats:** 1,902 views · 198 likes · **10.46% ER** · still growing today  
**Format:** Genuine emotional reaction to discovering the app. Shows it being used organically. No script energy — pure discovery.  
**Why it works:** The discovery emotion is highly shareable. Organic feel signals authenticity to the algorithm. The video is still gaining daily traction — proof the algorithm is distributing it.  
**Replicating with:** Bethany Vasquez (scale this format immediately), new onboarding creators.

---

### Format 4 — Educational Hook + App as Answer
**Best example:** @avaskincareadvice (Ava Mackenzie, IG)  
**Hook:** *"The best way to clear your skin overnight"* / *"The best way to determine if your skincare products will actually work"*  
**Stats:** 1,813 views + 1,517 views (consistent IG performer)  
**Format:** Educational promise in the hook → app demo fulfills the promise. Value-first approach.  
**Why it works:** Educational content drives saves (the most algorithm-positive metric). Hook promises a result without overselling.  
**Replicating with:** Ava Mackenzie (sustain volume), Anna Mauszycki (educational angle fits her style).

---

### Format 5 — Narrative Reframe: "My Skin Wasn't Bad, It Was the Products"
**Best example:** @annamaui.aestheticz (Anna Mauszycki, IG)  
**Hook:** *"Turns out my skin wasn't 'bad'… I just kept using products that didn't fit my skin 😭💀"*  
**Stats:** 1,403 views · strong emotional resonance in caption  
**Format:** Reframes the root cause of bad skin from personal failure → product mismatch → app reveals the truth. Conversational, relatable caption style.  
**Why it works:** Removes self-blame (relief-based hook). Very relatable for anyone who's tried multiple products with no results. Positions SkinQueens as the truth-teller, not just a tool.  
**Replicating with:** Anna Mauszycki (double down), Lavanna Rising (conversational style fits).

## Top 5 Competitor Formats — What's Working in the Market

Fresh research (June 1, 2026) on OnSkin, Lóvi, and SkinSort — the three direct competitors to SkinQueens. These formats have the highest proven virality and are the playbooks we're targeting in Week 5.

> **The SkinQueens moat:** None of these formats leverage the TikTok video share mechanic — the ability to share a TikTok directly into the app to analyze its products. That feature is completely unique to SkinQueens and is the most underused differentiator in our campaign. Week 5 corrects this.

---

### 🥇 Competitor Format 1 — Talking Head + Paid Partnership Disclosure
**Brand:** OnSkin  
**Creator:** @itz_justlola (Lola Winters)  
**Hook:** *"Definitely recommend the app onskin"*  
**Performance:** **41,000+ likes** — the highest-performing UGC piece across all competitors  
**Format:** Creator talks straight to camera, recommends the app with genuine energy, paid partnership disclosed upfront, shows the app briefly. Under 30 seconds.  
**Why it works:** The #paid disclosure doesn't hurt — it signals confidence. Energy is high, script is minimal. This is the same format Elise is already using for SkinQueens. **The 41K likes prove this is the ceiling we should be targeting.**  
**For SkinQueens Week 5:** Same format, add the TikTok share mechanic as the app demo — 10-second screen record of sharing a viral skincare TikTok into SkinQueens.

---

### 🥈 Competitor Format 2 — Safety Score Reveal / Suspense Hook
**Brand:** OnSkin (official @onskin.app + @klaudia_kobiela)  
**Hook:** *"How toxic your products are?"* / *"Check if your skin care is safe 🧴☑️"*  
**Performance:** 4,300+ likes across multiple videos; used consistently by OnSkin's own account  
**Format:** Creator scans a product → holds the moment before the score reveals → reacts to the result. The reveal is the content.  
**Why it works:** Suspense = watch time. Fear-based angle ("is this toxic?") taps into a universal skincare anxiety. The moment of reveal is shareable.  
**For SkinQueens Week 5:** Adapt to the share mechanic — *"I shared a viral TikTok to SkinQueens to see if those products are actually safe for my skin…"* → reveal of the match scores.

---

### 🥉 Competitor Format 3 — GRWM + App Integration
**Brand:** OnSkin  
**Creator:** @esmarianacruz (makeup artist)  
**Hook:** *"GRWM using @OnSkin"*  
**Performance:** ~4,300 likes; strong completion rate signal  
**Format:** Get Ready With Me routine with the app naturally integrated — creator checks product ingredients via the app as they apply them. App is a prop in a routine, not the centerpiece.  
**Why it works:** GRWM is the highest-performing format in beauty TikTok consistently. The app is integrated without being forced. Makeup artist credibility adds authority.  
**For SkinQueens Week 5:** GRWM where creator shares each product's TikTok into SkinQueens before applying it. Real-time match check during a morning/night routine.

---

### Competitor Format 4 — "This App Is Crazy!!" / Astonishment Demo
**Brand:** Lóvi  
**Creator:** @fitfashionistarock  
**Hook:** *"This skincare check app is crazy!!"*  
**Performance:** ~3,200 likes; strong comments asking "what app?"  
**Format:** Exclamation hook creates expectation → live demo of the scan → genuine reaction to results. Short, high energy.  
**Why it works:** "Crazy" creates a curiosity gap. The demo is the proof. Comment section fills with "what app is this?" which drives more organic reach.  
**For SkinQueens Week 5:** Apply to the TikTok scan mechanic — *"This AI app analyzed the skincare TikTok I was about to buy from and told me which products are actually for my skin 😱"*

---

### Competitor Format 5 — "Finally" / The Routine Fix
**Brand:** Lóvi  
**Creator:** @aasthaetics  
**Hook:** *"Finally following a good skincare routine"*  
**Performance:** ~1,100 likes; high engagement rate  
**Format:** "Finally" signals a long struggle finally resolved → the app is what made the difference → shows the corrected routine.  
**Why it works:** "Finally" implies accumulated frustration that's now over. Extremely relatable. The emotional arc (struggle → solution) is the strongest narrative pattern in UGC.  
**For SkinQueens Week 5:** *"Finally stopped wasting money on skincare that wasn't made for my skin"* → SkinQueens reveals the mismatch → corrected routine.
