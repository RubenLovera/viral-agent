# Warm-up Guide
**TikTok & Instagram — SkinQueens**
*Duration: 3 days before first post*

---

## Why This Matters

The algorithm decides in the first few hours whether your account is a real skincare user or a bot. If it doesn't see consistent, organic skincare behavior from day one, your content will be buried — even if the video is excellent.

Three days of intentional activity train the FYP to serve you skincare content. That means your future SkinQueens posts land in front of the right audience: people already deep in SkinTok who are frustrated with conflicting advice and wasting money on products that don't work for their skin.

Skip this step and you're posting into a void.

## 🛡️ 3. Rules to Avoid the Shadowban

**1. Do not scroll fast.**
Watch each video to the end — or at minimum, close to the end. Rapidly skipping through videos is the clearest bot signal there is. The algorithm tracks average watch time per session. Slow down.

**2. Quality over quantity in comments.**
5 specific comments that prove you watched the video are worth more than 50 fire emojis. If a comment could have been posted without watching the video, it doesn't count. Write like you have a real opinion.

**3. Spread activity across the day.**
Do not do your 30 minutes in one sitting. Open the app 3–4 times throughout the day: morning, midday, afternoon, and optionally evening. This mirrors normal usage patterns and avoids session-length anomalies that flag automation.

**4. Use the full platform — not just the FYP.**
Search keywords actively. Click on sounds and see what other videos use them. Explore hashtags. TikTok and Instagram reward accounts that use the platform the way it was designed. Only scrolling the FYP passively is a weaker signal.

**5. Do not follow more than 15–20 accounts per day.**
Aggressive mass-following triggers spam filters, especially on new accounts. Stick to 5–10 quality follows per day: real creators in the skincare niche who post consistently.

**6. Do not post before the 3 days are complete.**
Even one post before the algorithm has locked in the niche can hurt the account's initial distribution. The niche signal needs to be established first. Wait for confirmation from your UGC Manager before uploading anything.

## 📲 4. Every Time You Post

Before and after every single post, spend at least 10 minutes interacting on the feed. No exceptions.

**Before posting:**
Browse the FYP / Home feed for 10 minutes. Like, comment, save videos. Watch at least 3 videos to the end. Your goal is to arrive at the moment of posting as an active, warm account — not a cold one.

**After posting:**
Do the same for another 10 minutes. This signals to the algorithm that the account is active at the exact moment the post goes live, which directly affects how widely the content gets pushed in its first hour.

Skipping this step is the single most common reason videos underperform in the first hour. The first 60 minutes of a post's life determine 70–80% of its total reach. Treat it accordingly.

## 🧴 5. Your Account Persona (Who You Are on This Account)

Your account represents a real person who is passionate about skincare, overwhelmed by conflicting TikTok advice, and has found a smarter way to filter products and decisions. Everything you consume and engage with during the warm-up — and beyond — should be authentic to this persona.

**Who you are:**
- You've spent real money on skincare products that didn't work for your skin
- You started checking ingredients after a bad reaction or a video that made you realize you had no idea what was in your products
- You follow skincare creators because you care about the science, not just the aesthetics
- You're the friend people DM before buying anything from SkinTok
- You've been deep in the SkinTok rabbit hole for months

**What your account engages with:**
- Ingredient deep-dives and explanations
- Routine reveals, audits, and "grading" videos
- "Products I regret buying" and "skincare waste" content
- Dupe comparisons and budget finds
- Reaction videos to viral skincare claims
- Before-and-after skin transformation content
- Sensitive skin and barrier repair content

**What your account does NOT engage with:**
- General beauty / makeup content unrelated to skincare (different niche — confuses the algorithm)
- Fashion, travel, or lifestyle content (only engage if there's a skincare angle)
- Clearly promotional brand account content
- Anything that takes your account signal outside the skincare niche
